Ethiopia: The roof of Africa

Ethiopia, boasting a rich history as one of the oldest civilizations and the only African nation to resist European colonization, stands as a unique cultural and geographic landscape with its highlands, ancient rock-hewn churches, and diverse ethnic groups.

Information Section

The Ethiopian king who defeated Italy at the Battle of Adwa in 1896 was Menelik II. This victory is a significant moment in Ethiopian history, as it marked one of an African nation successfully resisted European colonization.

the Adey Abeba flower serves as a natural reminder of the Ethiopian calendar and the changing seasons, representing both the beauty of nature and the joy of new beginnings.

The Gelada monkey are sometimes called "bleeding-heart monkeys" or "gelada baboons" due to their reddish patch of skin on the chest, but they are actually a distinct species within the monkey family.

Lalibela's rock-hewn churches stand as a powerful symbol of Ethiopian faith, artistry, and ingenuity. They continue to inspire and amaze visitors from around the world.
